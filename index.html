About Us

  • Accurate explanation of sexual and asexual reproduction:
    Students clearly explain the processes of sexual and asexual reproduction, identify key differences between them, and describe how each type of reproduction affects genetic variation and offspring characteristics.
  • Correct use of genetics and reproduction vocabulary:
    Students consistently and accurately use key vocabulary terms (reproduction, heredity, gene, allele, chromosome, dominant, recessive, genotype, phenotype) in written and oral explanations, demonstrating clear understanding of meaning and context.
  • Accurate construction and interpretation of Punnett squares:
    Students correctly construct Punnett squares for simple genetic crosses, determine possible genotypes and phenotypes, and interpret probability outcomes using appropriate scientific reasoning.
  • Clear explanation of genotype–phenotype relationships:
    Students explain how genotypes determine phenotypes, describe the role of dominant and recessive alleles, and use examples to show how different genetic combinations result in observable traits.
  • Logical connection between reproduction, genetics, and variation using evidence:
    Students explain how reproductive processes influence genetic variation within populations, supporting explanations with models, examples, or data rather than personal opinions.
