Today, a big part of the world’s population is bilingual or multilingual rather than just monolingual. In a survey conducted by the European Commission in 2006, 56% of respondents reported being able to speak a language other than their mother tongue.

In addition to facilitating cross-cultural communication, bilingualism positively affects cognitive abilities. Research shows improved attention, task-switching capacity, faster stroke recovery, and delayed onset of dementia in multilingual individuals.
